My name is Tin Ma and I am currently a first-year psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. As a young and fervent student, I am excited to learn more about the human brain and extend my knowledge on the major, and ultimately grasp the ability to help people with that knowledge. Other than psychology, my secondary love in life is photography. My passion for the art began in my sophomore year of college when my friend convinced me to join the school journalism team with the assignment of taking photos and providing weekly updates for students at the school in the form of a newspaper. Though the course required mastery in other tasks than photography, I could not keep my hand off the camera. Swiftly, I learned the tips and tricks of the camera, surprisingly surpassing peers who were, in the beginning, much more skilled than I was. My experiences consist of my education and my internships: passing my high school advanced placement psychology class as well as the advanced placement test with a five, claiming psychology as my major in college, and multiple internships that I am currently attending. Though my experience is limited, my yearning passion to pursue psychology as my life-long career stands impenetrable.The internships include me shadowing therapists during their sessions and learning different techniques in order to accommodate different types of patients. Through these experiences, I have learned that every single patient has different issues and requires different types of techniques that make them feel as though their feelings are valid. As a psychology major, a major that emphasizes the importance of individuality of a personsâ€™ emotions, this is one of the first and most important lessons to learn.

I am a first-year, and because of that, I have yet to begin specializing in a certain field of psychology; but for now, I am leaning towards pursuing a career in being a family therapist, helping families through issues between one another as well as sole patients who seek out mental help from a professional. I began pursuing this career because of how much I loved helping friends and family with issues that they had. Whether they were happy or sad, I cherished being the person that they came to, and thus, sparking my love for psychology. Photography, on the other hand, is more of an abstract dream or a side hustle, if you will. Everytime I look through the lens of a camera, I find myself amazed at the scenery around me; So much so that I have created a small website in which people can book a photoshoot with me as their photographer. I am looking for job opportunities both in the field of psychology and the art of photography.
